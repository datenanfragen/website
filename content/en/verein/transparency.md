## 3. Information on tax privileges

As per the latest [exemption notice](https://static.dacdn.de/docs/freistellungsbescheid-2025-11-10.pdf) of the Braunschweig-Wilhelmstraße tax office (tax number 14/209/11640) dated November 10, 2025 for the assessment period 2022 to 2024, we are exempt from corporate taxes as per §&nbsp;5(1)(9) of the German Corporation Tax Act (KStG) and from trade taxes as per §&nbsp;3(6) of the German Trade Tax Act (GewStG) for the advancement of:

- science and research (§&nbsp;52(2)(1)(1) of the German Fiscal Code (AO))
- consumer counselling and consumer protection (§&nbsp;52(2)(1)(16) AO)
- democratic government (§&nbsp;52(2)(1)(24) AO)

Compliance with the statutory requirements under §§ 51, 59, 60, and 61 AO was separately determined by the Braunschweig-Wilhelmstraße tax office (tax number 14/209/11640) in the [assessment notice](https://static.dacdn.de/docs/feststellungsbescheid-2025-10-29.pdf) as per § 60a AO dated October 29, 2025. In accordance with our constitution, we advance:

- science and research (§&nbsp;52(2)(1)(1) AO)
- upbringing, adult education and vocational training (§&nbsp;52(2)(1)(7) AO)
- consumer counselling and consumer protection (§&nbsp;52(2)(1)(16) AO)
- democratic government (§&nbsp;52(2)(1)(24) AO)

## 11. Compensated lectures

Our {{< link slug="verein/board" text="board members" >}} work on a strictly voluntary basis and receive no payment from the association for their work. In some cases, however, members of the association can receive compensation from external organizations for lectures on topics that concern the association. Of course, no funds of the association are used for such compensated lectures, they are the private responsibility of the respective members.

The board has to publicly document lectures here for which board members have received compensation from external organizations. This is stipulated in our [lecture policy (German)](https://static.dacdn.de/docs/legal/vortragsordnung.pdf).

## 12. External grants that members have received

The association benefits when members apply for external grants on their own to fund their work on our projects. Awarded grants are documented here as per our [funding policy (German)](https://static.dacdn.de/docs/legal/foerdermittelordnung.pdf). The listed grants did not go to the association, but are the sole responsibility of the respective members.

### 2024

* The Federal Ministry of Education and Research funded the work of the Lorenz Sieben and Benjamin Altpeter GbR on the [“Tweasel for Web”](https://prototypefund.de/project/tweasel-for-web/) project with 47,481.00&nbsp;€ between September 2024 and February 2025 as part of the Prototype Fund (grant number 01IS24S28). The purpose of the project was to expand the Tweasel tools for analyzing tracking on websites and to develop a browser addon as a counterpart to the Tweasel platform for mobile apps. In the course of the project, an analysis of the top 10,000 websites in Germany was conducted, and the results were published via the [request database](https://data.tweasel.org/data/datasets/web-monkey-september-2024) and as an [open dataset](https://zenodo.org/records/13990110). Based on this, the [TrackHAR library](https://github.com/tweaselORG/TrackHAR) was extended for the web, with many new adapters added. Finally, a prototype of the [addon](https://github.com/tweaselORG/addon) was developed, which can analyze the network traffic of websites in an isolated context for tracking data transmissions and generate complaints based on this analysis.  
  [Project report (German)](https://static.bn.al/doc/abschlussbericht-ptf16-tweaselforweb.pdf)

### 2023

* The NLnet Foundation has funded the work of the Lorenz Sieben and Benjamin Altpeter GbR and Carolin Gaus on the [“Tracking weasel”](https://nlnet.nl/project/TrackingWeasel/) project with 48,750.00 € between January 2023 and June 2024 through the NGI0 Entrust Fund with financial support from the European Commission’s Next Generation Internet program (grant number 2022-08-061). The purpose of the project was to develop tools and libraries for the automatic analysis of data protection violations caused by tracking in mobile apps, as well as to develop a platform to make this infrastructure accessible to users. As part of the project, the [Tweasel libraries](https://docs.tweasel.org/) [appstraction](https://github.com/tweaselORG/appstraction), [cyanoacrylate](https://github.com/tweaselORG/cyanoacrylate), [TrackHAR](https://github.com/tweaselORG/TrackHAR), [ReportHAR](https://github.com/tweaselORG/ReportHAR), and [parse-tunes](https://github.com/tweaselORG/parse-tunes), as well as the [Tweasel CLI](https://github.com/tweaselORG/cli), were developed and presented at various events. Furthermore, an [open database of tracking requests](https://data.tweasel.org/) was published, and a prototype of the [platform](https://github.com/tweaselORG/platform) was developed.  
  *Project report to follow*

### 2022

* The German Federal Ministry of Education and Research funded the work of the Lorenz Sieben und Benjamin Altpeter GbR on the [“An app for Datenanfragen.de”](https://prototypefund.de/project/eine-app-fuer-datenanfragen-de/) project with 29,426.00&nbsp;€ between March 2022 and August 2022 through the Prototype Fund (grant number 01IS22S20). The purpose of the project was to evaluate whether exercising one’s privacy rights could be further simplified with a native app. In the course of the project, the existing code base of the website was also refactored and modernised, a new, more intuitive interface for the request generator was implemented, the “My requests” feature was expanded, and a generator for admonitions and complaints was developed.  
  [Project report (German)](https://static.bn.al/doc/abschlussbericht-ptf11-dade-app.pdf)
